Description: Essentially an elevated bench press, but the elevation affects the muscle being targeted.
Pro Tip: Common mistakes people tend to make when exercising with an incline bench press are: setting the bench at a high incline (over 50 degrees), arching their backs, bouncing the bar off their chest, and locking of elbows. Try to avoid making the above errors by keeping good form and keeping the incline of the bench at below 50 degrees. Finally, the position of your thumb is important. Grip the bar with your thumbs in opposition to your fingers, this is a more natural grip and safer for lifting.
Muscles Used: The upper chest is targeted by the incline, as well as shoulders and triceps. It is not uncommon to use dumbbells in junction with or instead of a barbell on an incline bench.

Description: While seated, the preacher curl bench helps you isolate and train your biceps. A perfect place for beginners to start biceps training by utilizing a lighter barbell and lifting up and down; slowly bringing the weight down.
Pro Tip: Sit with your underarms at the top of the edge and your triceps resting on the preacher bench pad. Whether you are doing one handed dumbbell exercises, such as hammer grips or one arm twists, or whether you are using a barbell, follow the above technique. Contrary to popular belief, this workout is not superior in building that biceps “peak.” But it does prevent you from cheating when working out biceps, and provides them with a precise contraction; separating the elbow movement from the rest of your body. Do not extend your arm too far, as this can result in injury. Also do not shift your body downwards toward the weight, just to get leverage and complete the curl. Most importantly, do not use too much weight and combine that with lowering the bar or dumbbell too far down.
Muscles Used: Biceps, brachialis, and forearms.

Without a doubt, the most common cardio piece of equipment in every gym. It’s basically a piece of machine designed for running in the same space via the use of a rotating belt. Another machine designed for cardio training is named elliptical. They work differently than treadmills, though. The motion done on these machines can be best compared to cross-country skiing, because they use dual-action handlebars. They are usually very compact, thus not taking much space. Besides cardio, working out with ellipticals increases stability, core strength and leg strength. There are a lot of manufactures who produce elliptical machines. Dumbbells are possibly the most known free weights in the world. They are used in weight training either individually, or in pairs. The handle is usually made of iron or steel and the weight parts are either made of iron, steel, cement or rubber. These dumbbells can also be adjustable, meaning you can change the desired weight on each dumbbell by unscrewing and re-screwing the weight plates from the handle. Adjustable dumbbells often come in sets, but are usually much more expensive than regular dumbbells. Either way it never hurts to have a pair ready at your disposal in your home gym.
